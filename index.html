The problem

For centuries, we have been burning fossil fuels to generate a huge amount of electricity for our consumption needs thus producing carbon emissions that will harm our planet Earth. This results in a gradual increase in the overall temperature of the earth's atmosphere causing Global Warming.

Admit it. At some point, you too complained that Singapore was too hot right?

Urban Heat Island Effect

Did you know that on a hot day, the roads in Singapore get up to as hot as 60 degrees Celsius? Thatâ€™s so much heat energy, and it increases the temperature around urban areas like cities.

Global Warming

Everyone knows Global Warming happens mainly due to burning of fossil fuels to generate electricity. The solution? simple, just use green energy! but how? With the currrent technology, using existing clean energy devices would not surfice in running a city. We need more clean energy devices.

Lack Of Space

Singapore is one of the smallest countries in the world, therefore, you can never see huge solar farms and windmill farms in Singapore because there is literally nowhere to place them due to lack of space. This poses as another challenge for countries like Singapore to go fully green.

The solution

When we create our products, our end goal was to create new ways to generate clean energy. This way, we can never run out of alternative optinons to go green!

Our Timeline

How it all started...

  • July 2019

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Our team was first formed as we are participating in Ikea Young Designers Award 2019. The theme of the competition was "zero waste challenge". We managed to win the merit prize and the people's choice prize by presenting our EcoRoad idea alongside with the first prototype as a proof of concept.

  • November 2019 - Today

    Turning possibility to reality

    Our project in Ikea caught attention of an NYC member who recommended us in joining in the Youth Action Challenge. The Youth Action Challenge is a competition between students to pitch their ideas to professionals in hopes of getting funding to kickstart their project. We decided to give this competition a shot and as of today, we are the top 12 finalist teams for the competition.

  • April 2020 - Today

    Final Year Project

    We decided to use this project as our FYP in Ngee Ann Polytechnic. This way, we are able to allocate more time to improving our products and refining on our ideas.
